Project description

Like many of my classmates that either had no interest in learning how to write their own websites, or better yet, had no time due to the workload many of us had already taken on, my portfolio and anything else web related was set up by helper sites like Wix.com. These woked well up until mobile had become almost a requirement. And I personally found most of their tools used at the time to the mobile side were often janky and, worse very time consuming.

A few years ago I was trying to prepare a presentation using Wix for the upcoming GDC in San Francisco to show off a game pitch I had been working on for about a year. At the same time, the super awesome boss I had then at my daytime job had gotten me a 5 minute window with the CEO of the company during a rare visit to do a second presentation for pitching my Lucky Strike San Franciso ~ Virtual Tour project you can view here on this site.

3 months went into both. The virtual tour I'd been setting up the desktop site for a while just in hope of getting 5 seconds, let alone 5 minutes. But it was when it came down to the mobile every would constantly go wrong. Even though the desktop versions did actually get completed, no other eyes but mine seen them, and neither presentation worked out.

Anyone that's been to GCD knows that if you don't have a booth you're either on the move and mingling on your feet, or find your way to an after party which is where you really want to be. Carrying a laptop around all day, or to a late night party, isn't the greatest situation. It was awkward, clumsy and made both presentions uninteresting. Especially when it came time to show my stuff to the CEO who definitely wanted it on the go.
