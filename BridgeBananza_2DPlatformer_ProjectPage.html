A short 2.5D Physcis Based Platformer that was a very amusing time killer, tasking players with the challenge of stacking the pieces provided to bridge the gaps, avoid a variety of obstacles and try to get the monkey to the giant banana.

Project description

As 1 of the 2 six week sprints during a prototyping class in grad school, we were all challenged to pitch a game idea and then break up into groups based on the pitches we each presented to each other. Most sacrificing their ideas to help bring someone elses to life.


There was two of us on this particular project, and probably my favorite project so far. The workload was split according to our desired fields, which worked out very nicely as, my partner Will Burke, was the better artist and knew his fair share of the codinng. Which allowed us the common ground we took advantage of when it came time integrate our two halves of the project.

My contributions

Level Design and Development

Design

For this project my aim was to fulfill 3 primary tasks.

- Metrics: Establish scale and movement parameters
- Difficulty curve: Designed and tested a series of level setups to find a challenging but fun experience in only a few short scenarios
- Bridge piece type/ quantity: Designed the types of pieces the player uses to build their bridge. Then, tested each level to determine the number of pieces it would take based on the intended difficulty curve

Typically my methods are kept simple during the early stages. Some graph page and imagination seem to be the recipe that works best to get things going.

As an extremely imaginative individual, I find it quite difficult to settle on any one thing, like favorites. Bias thoughts tend to hinder the creative process, and deprives one of all the extraordinarily beautiful experiences that come with loving all diversities throughout life and beyond.
